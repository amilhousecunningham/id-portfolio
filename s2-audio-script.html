Audio Script



  • Creating a budget for the first time can be daunting! But don’t worry; you can become a budgeting pro in less than three minutes. Simply follow the steps outlined in this video, and you'll never stress about budgeting again. Let’s get started!

  • Since there is a difference between gross and net income, you’ll first need to figure out your net income.

  • What's net income you wonder? It’s the amount of money you’re paid after taxes and deductions. Are you a little confused? Well, that’s okay. Let's look at an example!

  • Alexandra’s gross income is $4,300 per month. After taxes and deductions, her net income is $3,650 a month. Notice there’s a $650 difference between her gross and net income. Does that clear things up for you?

  • If not, here’s a tip, always look at your pay stub! Your net income should be listed.

  • Alright, you’re ready for the next step! Tracking your expenses is extremely important. You can do this by making a list of your fixed and variable costs.

  • Fixed costs include expenses like rent and car payments. These costs are typically predictable and do not change from month to month.

  • Variable costs include expenses, such as food entertainment. These costs typically fluctuate, meaning increase or decrease over time. Now you have a better overview of spending, let's move on!

  • We’re at the final and most important step – making a plan! You need to take a close look at your fixed and variable costs. Are you okay with these expenses, or do you want to increase or reduce your spending?

  • If you’re a little stuck, I advise using the 50/30/20 rule. This rule divides your net income into three categories – needs, wants, and savings. With this rule, 50% of your net income goes towards your needs, 30% towards your wants, and 20% towards savings. A little confused? Let’s look back at our example with Alexandra.

  • Remember, her net income is $3,650. If she uses the 50/30/20 rule, that means $1825 of her income will go towards her needs, $1095 will go towards her wants, and $730 towards savings. Please note it's important to adjust your spending to fit your budget as needed.


  • Tip, there are free 50/30/20 budget templates online which makes planning easier. For example, visit cleavergirlfinance.com.  

  • You did it! You’re a budgeting pro! Congrats!

Planning Document


Client Information

Name of Client: Shalonda Clark

Title of Client: Teacher

Organization of Client: DeKalb County School District


Needs Assessment
  • Learning Need: Mrs. Clark shared that students need support in the area of reading. She would like students to develop their comprehension skills by asking and answering questions about various texts to demonstrate their overall understanding. Mrs. Clark also wants students to practice referring to texts to find evidence or support for their answers.
  • Audience The learners are third graders, specifically English Language Learners (ELL). They are eight or nine years old and of Hispanic descent. Spanish is their first language, and English is their second language. Their interests include learning and spending time with family and friends.
  • Content Area: The instruction will focus on reading, with an emphasis on comprehension.

Learning Objectives
  • Objective 1: Students will be able to ask and answer questions to demonstrate their understanding of a text with at least 80% accuracy.
  • Objective 2: Students will be able to refer to a text as the basis for their answers and questions with at least 80% accuracy.

Standards

  • The instruction is designed to address the skills and concepts outlined in ELAGSE3RL1, which states that students should be able to ask and answer questions to demonstrate understanding of a text, while referring explicitly to the text as the basis for the answers.

Learning Activities

  • The learning activities will be divided into five sections. In section one, students will be introduced to the standard and lesson objectives. In section two, students will watch a video about asking and answering questions, and they will complete two practice activities to reinforce the information presented in the video. In the third section, students will dive deeper and explore how to reference a text and support their answers with evidence. In the fourth section, students will complete a ReadWorks passage, and lastly students will engage in self-reflection.

Multimedia Resources

  • Asking and Answering Questions About a Story: Reading Literature
  • Comprehension: Answering Questions
  • The Ants and the Grasshopper

Accessibility

  • The videos in the modules have closed-captioning and options to adjust the playback speed. Furthermore, Rise 360, a cloud-based authoring tool, has built-in accessibility features that meet 508 compliance requirements. For non-accessibile components, such as sorting and matching pairs activities, a text-based response has been provided that summarizes the content.

Copyright and Fair Use

  • References and links are provided at the end of the course to ensure proper citation of resources and compliance with copyright and fair use requirements.

Assessment

  • In section four of the module, students will complete a formal assessment. To demonstrate mastery, students must achieve a minimum score of 80%. The activities in the other sections of the course are informal assessments. Students are expected to attempt all the activities.

Project Coordination and Communication

  • Since teachers are on summer vacation, Mrs. Clark and I communicated electronically. She informed me about her students' needs, and I kept her up to date on the progress of developing the course.
